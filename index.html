02 — Logo System

Wordmark, Monogram & Geometric Mark

The EchoFour identity is built from three elements: an all-caps wordmark with weighted anchors, a staggered E4 monogram that breaks the frame, and the approved staggered canvases geometric mark. Together they form a system that works from favicon to storefront.

Wordmark — ECHOFOUR

The full name set in all caps with the E and F scaled slightly larger to create visual anchors. This treatment gives the wordmark a structured rhythm without splitting the name into two words. Use for headers, print materials, and formal brand contexts.

Monogram — Staggered E4

A solid canvas rectangle sits at center. A geometric E flanks the top-left corner and a geometric 4 flanks the bottom-right corner, both overlapping the canvas edges. Where the letterforms cross the canvas, they reverse to the canvas color, creating a two-tone interlocking effect. Use for favicon, social avatar, emboss stamp, and watermark.

Primary Geometric Mark — Staggered Canvases

Four squares cascading diagonally, like prints being pulled from a plate. The sharp rectangular edges reference the canvas. The layering shows the process: a painting becoming a print, technology meeting craft. This is the approved geometric mark direction, now paired with the new ECHOFOUR wordmark and staggered E4 monogram. Next revision: integrate E at the rear canvas position and 4 at the front canvas position to unify the geometric mark with the monogram language.

Clear Space & Minimum Size

Maintain clear space equal to the cap-height of the "E" on all sides of the wordmark. Minimum size: 100px wide for wordmark, 24px for monogram/mark. The staggered E4 monogram is intended for use as an emboss die on the physical prints. The staggered canvases mark works at all scales from app icon to storefront signage.

06 — Tone of Voice

Warm. Intentional. With Purpose.

EchoFour speaks like a passionate curator who found something worth sharing — not a gallery institution, not a hype brand. The tone should make collectors feel like they're part of something being built, and make galleries feel like partners in elevating artists who deserve a wider audience.

Do: Purposeful Warmth

Write like someone who found a great artist and wants to tell you about them. Confident but human. Let the mission come through — we use technology to give traditional painters a modern platform.

"We found an artist in a studio overseas. His painting became our next print."

Don't: Hype or Cold Institutional

Two extremes to avoid: streetwear hype (urgency, all-caps, fire emojis) and stuffy gallery speak (cold, distant, impersonal). We're neither Supreme nor MoMA. We're building something new.

"DROPPING SOON!!" / "Now exhibiting in our collection."

Do: Show the Journey

Every print has a story: the artist we found, their tradition, and the process of turning a painting into a tactile object through modern production. Show how technology elevates the craft — don't hide behind it.

"Lena paints in the Impressionist tradition. We turned her latest canvas into something you can feel under your fingertips."

Don't: Empty Art Speak

Avoid jargon that sounds impressive but says nothing. Also avoid being so minimal that you lose the human element. The brand has a point of view — share it.

"A dialogue between materiality and the ephemeral nature of being."

Key Vocabulary

Use

09 — Photography & Art Direction

Show the People.
Show the Process.

Photography should make people feel the story behind the print. The artist in their studio. Hands on the embossing press. The texture you can almost feel through the screen. Real people, real craft, real spaces — not floating in a white void.

Style 1 The Artist Artist in their studio. Painting in progress. Natural light. Human, not posed. This is who we're lifting up.
Style 2 The Process The embossing press. Hands working. Painting becoming print. Documentary, not polished. Show the technology meeting the craft.
Style 3 The Print Close-up of embossed texture. Raking sidelight. In a real space, on a real wall. Make people want to touch it.
